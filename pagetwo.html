Mental Health Disorders

Depression

Depression is a mood disorder characterized by persistent feelings of sadness, hopelessness, and loss of interest in activities.

Symptoms may include changes in appetite, sleep patterns, and energy levels.

Treatment options include therapy, medication, and lifestyle changes.



Anxiety

Anxiety is a mental health disorder characterized by excessive and persistent worry, fear, or anxiety.

Symptoms may include rapid heartbeat, sweating, and trembling.

Treatment options include therapy, medication, and relaxation techniques.



Bipolar Disorder

Bipolar disorder is a mental health disorder characterized by extreme mood swings, ranging from manic highs to depressive lows.

Symptoms may include changes in energy, activity level, and sleep patterns.

Treatment options include therapy, medication, and lifestyle changes.



Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD)

PTSD is a mental health disorder that develops after a person experiences a traumatic event.

Symptoms may include flashbacks, nightmares, and avoidance of triggers.

Treatment options include therapy, medication, and support groups.



Schizophrenia

Schizophrenia is a mental health disorder characterized by hallucinations, delusions, and disorganized thinking.

Symptoms may include changes in behavior, mood, and cognitive function.

Treatment options include therapy, medication, and social support.
